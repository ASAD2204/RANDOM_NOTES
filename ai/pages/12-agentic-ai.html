Agentic AI

Autonomous systems that plan, execute tools, evaluate outcomes, and iterate.

▶ Agentic capabilities

  • Planning: decompose goals into executable subtasks.
  • Reasoning: evaluate alternatives under constraints.
  • Tool usage: call APIs, search systems, code tools, and databases.
  • Memory: preserve progress and intermediate outcomes.
  • Feedback loops: verify results and self-correct.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Agentic AI extends single-shot generation into closed-loop decision processes. The core theoretical shift is from “answer generation” to “policy execution.” The system repeatedly chooses actions based on evolving state. Reliable agents require bounded autonomy: explicit stopping rules, tool permissioning, and verifiable intermediate checkpoints.

  • Planning horizon: short plans are safer; long plans need frequent replanning.
  • Observability: traces of thought/action are essential for debugging and trust.
  • Control: policy constraints prevent unsafe or unproductive behavior.

🌟 Engineering challenge

Agent quality depends on robust tool interfaces, deterministic guardrails, clear stop criteria, and strong failure-recovery strategies.
