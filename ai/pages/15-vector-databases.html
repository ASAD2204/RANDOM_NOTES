Vector Databases

Infrastructure for high-dimensional embedding storage and approximate similarity search.

▶ Why vector DBs?

Semantic retrieval relies on nearest-neighbor search in embedding space, which traditional B-tree style indexing is not optimized for.

▶ Concepts

  • Embeddings represent semantic meaning as dense vectors.
  • Similarity metrics: cosine, dot product, and L2 distance.
  • ANN indexing (HNSW/IVF/PQ) trades exactness for speed and scale.
  • Metadata filtering narrows candidate sets before ranking.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Vector search quality depends on embedding model quality, index hyperparameters, and query formulation. Retrieval systems optimize both recall (finding relevant items) and latency. In production, teams tune chunking, embedding refresh cadence, and ANN index settings to maintain relevance as corpora evolve.

  • Index trade-off: higher recall usually increases compute and latency.
  • Semantic drift: embeddings may need re-generation after model updates.
  • Hybrid retrieval: combining lexical + vector often improves robustness.

▶ Ecosystem

FAISS (library), Pinecone (managed), ChromaDB (developer-friendly), Weaviate (rich schema and hybrid retrieval).
