▶ Theory Deep Dive
AI system design is an end-to-end optimization problem across quality, latency, cost, safety, and maintainability. A high-performing model can still fail as a product if observability, fallback logic, and data governance are weak. The core design principle is separation of concerns: inference, retrieval, orchestration, and policy should be independently evolvable.
  • Product requirements: define measurable success metrics before architecture selection.
  • Failure design: include graceful degradation when model confidence is low.
  • Feedback loops: collect user corrections to improve future versions.
