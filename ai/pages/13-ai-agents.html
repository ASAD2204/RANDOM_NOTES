AI Agents

Agent taxonomies, coordination patterns, and real-world deployment models.

▶ Agent types

  • Reactive: direct response to current observation.
  • Model-based: maintain internal world state.
  • Goal-based: select actions that satisfy explicit goals.
  • Utility-based: optimize utility under trade-offs.
  • Learning agents: improve policy from experience feedback.

▶ Single vs multi-agent

Single-agent: one planner/executor loop
Multi-agent: specialized roles (researcher, coder, reviewer) coordinate tasks

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Agent design is an architecture choice over autonomy, controllability, and coordination overhead. Single-agent setups are simpler and easier to debug. Multi-agent systems can improve specialization and throughput but introduce communication and consistency complexity. In interviews, justify agent topology based on task decomposition, reliability requirements, and operational observability.

  • Role clarity: each agent should have clear responsibilities and interfaces.
  • Conflict resolution: voting, arbitration, or hierarchical control may be required.
  • Evaluation: measure both task success and coordination efficiency.
