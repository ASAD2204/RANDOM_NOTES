Normalization

1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF with practical decomposition examples.

📚 Why normalization?

Reduce redundancy and prevent update/insert/delete anomalies.

1NF

Rule: atomic values, no repeating groups.
Example: split phone1, phone2 into separate rows/table.

2NF

Rule: in 1NF + no partial dependency on composite key.
Example:
OrderItem(order_id, product_id, product_name, qty)
product_name depends only on product_id -> move to Product table.

3NF

Rule: in 2NF + no transitive dependency.
Example:
Employee(emp_id, dept_id, dept_name)
dept_name depends on dept_id, not emp_id -> split department table.

BCNF

Rule: every determinant must be a candidate key.
Stricter than 3NF, resolves some anomalies still present in 3NF.

🌟 Interview Tips

  • 3NF is commonly sufficient in OLTP systems.
  • Denormalization may be intentional for read-heavy analytics.
