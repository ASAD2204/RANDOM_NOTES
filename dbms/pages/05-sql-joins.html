SQL Joins

INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, FULL, SELF joins with example tables and outputs.

📋 Sample tables

employees(emp_id, emp_name, dept_id)
(1,Asha,10) (2,Ravi,20) (3,Meera,10) (4,Karan,NULL)

departments(dept_id, dept_name)
(10,Engineering) (20,HR) (30,Finance)

▶ INNER JOIN

SELECT e.emp_name, d.dept_name
FROM employees e
INNER JOIN departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id;

Output:
Asha  Engineering
Ravi  HR
Meera Engineering

▶ LEFT JOIN

SELECT e.emp_name, d.dept_name
FROM employees e
LEFT JOIN departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id;

Output:
Asha  Engineering
Ravi  HR
Meera Engineering
Karan NULL

▶ RIGHT JOIN

SELECT e.emp_name, d.dept_name
FROM employees e
RIGHT JOIN departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id;

Output:
Asha  Engineering
Ravi  HR
Meera Engineering
NULL  Finance

▶ FULL JOIN

SELECT e.emp_name, d.dept_name
FROM employees e
FULL OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id;

Output includes all rows from both sides:
Asha Engineering
Ravi HR
Meera Engineering
Karan NULL
NULL Finance

▶ SELF JOIN

employees(emp_id, emp_name, manager_id)
(1,Asha,NULL) (2,Ravi,1) (3,Meera,1)

SELECT e.emp_name AS employee, m.emp_name AS manager
FROM employees e
LEFT JOIN employees m ON e.manager_id = m.emp_id;

Output:
Asha  NULL
Ravi  Asha
Meera Asha

🌟 Interview Tips

  • Know that FULL OUTER JOIN may need UNION workaround in MySQL.
  • Always write join condition explicitly with table aliases.
