CPU Scheduling

FCFS, SJF, Round Robin, Priority, and Multilevel Queue with example timeline.

📋 Example processes

Process  Arrival  Burst  Priority
P1       0        5      2
P2       1        3      1
P3       2        8      3
P4       3        6      2

FCFS (First-Come First-Serve)

Non-preemptive. Runs by arrival order.

Gantt (approx):
0 | P1 | 5 | P2 | 8 | P3 | 16 | P4 | 22

SJF (Shortest Job First)

Chooses shortest burst among ready processes (non-preemptive version).

Round Robin (RR)

Preemptive with fixed quantum q. Fair for time-sharing systems.

q=2 example sequence:
P1 P2 P3 P4 P1 P2 P3 P4 ... until completion

Priority Scheduling

Highest priority process first (preemptive or non-preemptive).

Multilevel Queue Scheduling

Separate queues (system, interactive, batch) each with own policy and fixed priority between queues.

📊 Metrics

  • Turnaround time = completion - arrival
  • Waiting time = turnaround - burst
  • Response time = first run - arrival

🌟 Interview Tips

  • Know convoy effect in FCFS.
  • SJF optimal average waiting but requires burst prediction.
  • RR too small quantum = too many context switches.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Scheduling is a policy decision over competing objectives: throughput, turnaround, response time, and fairness. No single algorithm is universally optimal.
