Process Synchronization

Critical section problem and synchronization primitives with classic examples.

📚 Critical section problem

Multiple threads/processes accessing shared data concurrently can cause race conditions.

Requirements

1) Mutual Exclusion
2) Progress
3) Bounded Waiting

Mutex and Semaphore

  • Mutex: lock/unlock for mutual exclusion.
  • Semaphore: integer with wait(P) and signal(V); binary/counting variants.

Peterson Algorithm (2 processes)

Uses flags + turn variable to ensure mutual exclusion in software (busy waiting).

Classic problems

  • Producer-Consumer (bounded buffer)
  • Dining Philosophers
Producer-Consumer idea:
Producer waits if buffer full.
Consumer waits if buffer empty.
Use semaphores: empty, full, mutex.

🌟 Interview Tips

  • Always mention race condition and critical section first.
  • Differentiate busy waiting spinlocks vs blocking synchronization.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Synchronization enforces correctness under concurrency. Race conditions arise when shared-state updates are not atomic; coordination primitives protect critical sections.
