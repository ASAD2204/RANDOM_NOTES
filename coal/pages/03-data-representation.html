Data Representation

Signed integers, two's complement, and floating-point basics.

▶ Signed integers and two's complement

In n-bit two's complement, range is -2^(n-1) to 2^(n-1)-1. MSB indicates sign in effect.

8-bit +13 = 00001101
Invert bits -> 11110010; +1 -> 11110011 = -13

Two's complement allows same adder hardware for positive and negative arithmetic.

▶ Overflow concept

Overflow in signed addition occurs when adding two positives gives negative or two negatives gives positive in fixed-width arithmetic.

▶ Floating point (IEEE 754 idea)

Value = (-1)^sign × 1.fraction × 2^(exponent-bias)
Single precision: 1 sign bit, 8 exponent bits, 23 fraction bits

Floating point represents huge range but has rounding error (e.g., 0.1 not exact in binary).
