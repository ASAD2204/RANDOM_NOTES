Sliding Window and Two Pointer Techniques

Linear-time optimization for subarray/substring and sorted-array problems.

📚 Concept

  • Sliding window: maintain dynamic range [l..r].
  • Two pointers: move two indices with invariant conditions.

💡 Analogy

A camera frame sliding across a long road while tracking best segment.

📄 Step-by-step (variable window)

for r in range(n):
  expand with a[r]
  while window invalid:
    shrink from left
  update answer

⏱ Complexity

Usually O(n) because each pointer moves at most n times.

✅ / ❌

  • ✅ Huge speed-up from O(n²) brute force to O(n).
  • ❌ Invariant design can be subtle.

⚠ Common Mistakes

  • Not updating left pointer correctly (infinite loops).
  • Forgetting to remove frequency when shrinking.
  • Using two pointers on unsorted data without right invariant.

🌟 Interview Tips

  • When asked “longest/shortest subarray/substring with condition,” think window first.
