OOP Best Practices

Guidelines for maintainable object-oriented design.

🌟 Design Guidelines

  • Prefer composition over inheritance unless subtype relation is strong.
  • Keep classes small and cohesive; one reason to change.
  • Hide fields; expose behavior-oriented APIs, not raw state mutators.
  • Program to interfaces/abstractions, not implementations.
  • Avoid god objects and deep inheritance trees.
  • Write tests at behavior boundaries (interfaces/services).
  • Document invariants and thread-safety assumptions.

⚠ Anti-patterns

  • Anemic domain model (all data, no behavior).
  • Tight coupling to concrete classes.
  • Excessive static/global state.
  • Leaky abstractions exposing internals.

Object-oriented design should be explained through abstraction boundaries, coupling/cohesion, and maintainability trade-offs.
