▶ Relationship Diagram Practice

Association:
[Teacher] -------- [Student]

Aggregation (weak ownership):
[Team] <>-------- [Player]
(Player may exist outside Team)

Composition (strong ownership):
[House] *--------- [Room]
(Room lifecycle tied to House)

▶ SOLID One-Glance Map

S  One class, one reason to change
O  Extend behavior without editing stable core
L  Child must preserve parent contract
I  Keep interfaces small and focused
D  Depend on abstractions, inject implementations

🌟 Whiteboard Interview Flow

  1. Draw entities and relationships first.
  2. Mark ownership boundaries (aggregation vs composition).
  3. Mark extension points via interfaces/abstract classes.
  4. State one edge case and one scaling strategy.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Object-oriented design should be explained through abstraction boundaries, coupling/cohesion, and maintainability trade-offs.
