Product-Based Company OOP Round

Typical pattern: deeper design trade-offs, extensibility, and architecture quality under change.

📚 What they usually test

  • Strong modeling under evolving requirements.
  • SOLID principles in concrete design decisions.
  • Use of abstractions, dependency injection, and testability thinking.
  • Handling edge cases, failure paths, and maintainability pressure.

▶ Common prompts

  1. Design Notification framework with channels, retries, and rate limits.
  2. Design extensible pricing engine with discounts and coupon stacking rules.
  3. Build ride-booking domain model with strategy-based fare calculation.
  4. Refactor a monolithic God class into clean service boundaries.

⚠ Common rejection reasons

  • Inheritance-heavy design where composition would be cleaner.
  • No extension path for new features (violates OCP).
  • Tight coupling to concrete classes; difficult to unit test.
  • No ownership model for state and lifecycle.

🌟 Scoring booster

  • State trade-off explicitly: simplicity now vs extensibility later.
  • Propose interface-driven boundaries and dependency injection.
  • Include one short refactor plan after initial version.

▶ Theory Deep Dive

Object-oriented design should be explained through abstraction boundaries, coupling/cohesion, and maintainability trade-offs.
